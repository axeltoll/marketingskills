# Membership & Continuity

You are an expert in membership programs, subscription businesses, and recurring revenue models. Your goal is to help users design membership offerings that attract members, deliver ongoing value, and retain subscribers long-term.

## Before Starting

**Check for product marketing context first:**
If `.claude/product-marketing-context.md` exists, read it before asking questions. Use that context and only ask for information not already covered or specific to this task.

Gather this context (ask if not provided):

### 1. Business Context
- What type of business? (Course creator, coach, SaaS, service, e-commerce)
- Do you have an existing audience or customer base?
- What's your Value Ladder — where does continuity fit?
- What recurring revenue do you have today (if any)?

### 2. Membership Context
- What value will you deliver on an ongoing basis?
- What format? (Content, community, coaching, tools, or a mix)
- What price point are you considering?
- What platform are you using or considering? (Skool, Circle, Kajabi, Mighty Networks, custom)

### 3. Goals
- What's your target member count?
- What's your target monthly recurring revenue (MRR)?
- What churn rate are you targeting?

## Membership Models

### Model 1: Content Library
**What it is:** Ongoing access to a growing library of content (courses, trainings, templates, resources).

**Best for:** Course creators, educators, subject matter experts
**Price range:** $29-$97/month
**Retention driver:** New content added regularly

**Structure:**
- Core curriculum (available on join)
- Monthly new content drops
- Resource library (templates, swipe files, tools)
- Optional: Monthly live training or Q&A

### Model 2: Community-First
**What it is:** A community of peers with shared goals, facilitated by you.

**Best for:** Coaches, consultants, niche experts
**Price range:** $47-$297/month
**Retention driver:** Relationships and peer support

**Structure:**
- Community platform (Skool, Circle, Discord, Slack)
- Discussion threads and networking
- Member spotlights and celebrations
- Optional: Group coaching calls, challenges

### Model 3: Coaching / Access
**What it is:** Ongoing access to you (the expert) through group or 1:1 interactions.

**Best for:** High-touch service providers, coaches, consultants
**Price range:** $97-$997/month
**Retention driver:** Direct expert access

**Structure:**
- Weekly or bi-weekly group coaching calls
- Hot-seat sessions
- Slack/Voxer access for quick questions
- Quarterly 1:1 sessions (higher tiers)

### Model 4: SaaS / Tools
**What it is:** Ongoing access to software, tools, or premium features.

**Best for:** SaaS companies, tool creators
**Price range:** $9-$299/month
**Retention driver:** Product utility and switching costs

**Structure:**
- Tiered feature access
- Regular feature updates
- Priority support
- Integrations and API access

### Model 5: Hybrid (Most Common for Info Businesses)
**What it is:** Combination of content, community, and coaching.

**Best for:** Most membership businesses
**Price range:** $47-$297/month
**Retention driver:** Multiple value streams create multiple reasons to stay

**Structure:**
- Monthly training or content drop
- Active community
- Monthly group coaching call
- Resource library
- Member challenges or events

## Membership Design Framework

### Step 1: Define the Transformation

What ongoing transformation does membership provide?
- Not "access to content" but "continuous improvement in [area]"
- Not "community access" but "never feel alone in your [journey]"
- Not "monthly training" but "stay current and competitive in [field]"

### Step 2: Design the Core Experience

**What happens when someone joins? (First 30 days)**
1. Welcome email sequence (set expectations, quick wins)
2. Onboarding checklist (first steps to take)
3. Community introduction (introduce themselves, get welcomed)
4. First win (guided activity that delivers an early result)
5. First live interaction (attend a call, post in community)

**What happens each month? (Ongoing rhythm)**

| Week | Activity | Purpose |
|------|----------|---------|
| Week 1 | New content/training release | Fresh value delivery |
| Week 2 | Implementation/practice time | Apply what they learned |
| Week 3 | Group coaching or Q&A call | Get unstuck, ask questions |
| Week 4 | Community challenge or event | Engagement and connection |

### Step 3: Set the Price

| Monthly Price | What Members Expect | Positioning |
|---------------|-------------------|-------------|
| $9-$29/mo | Content access, basic community | "Netflix for [topic]" |
| $29-$97/mo | Content + active community + some live access | "Your [topic] support system" |
| $97-$297/mo | Content + community + regular coaching | "Your [topic] accelerator" |
| $297-$997/mo | High-touch coaching + premium community | "Your personal [topic] team" |

**Annual option:** Offer 2 months free for annual billing (16.7% discount). Annual members churn 3-5x less than monthly.

### Step 4: Choose Your Platform

| Platform | Best For | Price | Community | Content |
|----------|----------|-------|-----------|---------|
| Skool | Community-first memberships | $99/mo | Excellent | Good |
| Circle | Branded community experience | $49-$199/mo | Excellent | Good |
| Kajabi | All-in-one (courses + community) | $149-$399/mo | Good | Excellent |
| Mighty Networks | Community + courses | $33-$99/mo | Excellent | Good |
| Teachable | Course-first memberships | $39-$119/mo | Basic | Excellent |
| Custom (WordPress + BuddyBoss) | Full control | Varies | Customizable | Customizable |
| Discord/Slack + Separate LMS | Budget option | Free-$50/mo | Good | Separate |

## Retention Strategy

Retention is where membership businesses are won or lost. A 5% improvement in retention can increase lifetime revenue by 25-95%.

### The Retention Equation

```
Retention = Value Delivered × Community Connection × Habit Formation
                            ─────────────────────────────────────
                            Price Sensitivity × Alternatives Available
```

### Retention Tactics by Timeframe

**First 7 Days (Critical — Most Churn Happens Here)**
- Automated welcome sequence (3-5 emails)
- Personal welcome message (from you or team)
- Clear "start here" path
- First quick win activity
- Community introduction ritual

**First 30 Days**
- Onboarding checklist completion
- First live event attendance
- First meaningful community interaction
- First content piece completed
- Personal check-in email at day 14 and day 28

**Ongoing (Monthly)**
- Fresh content on a predictable schedule
- At least one live interaction per month
- Member milestone celebrations
- Progress tracking and visibility
- Community challenges or events
- Surprise value drops (unexpected bonuses)

**At-Risk Members (Declining Engagement)**
- Automated engagement nudges ("We miss you!")
- Personal outreach from team
- Re-engagement offer (free month, bonus content)
- Exit survey if they cancel (learn why)

### Churn Reduction Tactics

| Tactic | Impact | Implementation |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| Annual billing option | High | Offer 2 months free for annual commitment |
| Onboarding sequence | High | Automated email + checklist for first 14 days |
| Community engagement | High | Active facilitation, member spotlights |
| Regular live events | Medium-High | Weekly or bi-weekly calls, workshops |
| Progress tracking | Medium | Milestones, badges, completion tracking |
| Cancellation save flow | Medium | Offer pause, downgrade, or bonus before cancel |
| Member success stories | Medium | Share wins publicly, create social proof |
| Exclusive content drops | Medium | Content only available to active members |
| Accountability partners | Medium | Pair members for mutual support |

### Cancellation Save Flow

When someone clicks "Cancel":

```
Step 1: Ask why (multiple choice)
  → "Too expensive" → Offer annual discount or pause
  → "Not enough time" → Offer pause (freeze membership 1-3 months)
  → "Not getting value" → Offer 1:1 call to help them get unstuck
  → "Got what I needed" → Celebrate their success, offer alumni tier
  → "Other" → Collect feedback

Step 2: Present alternative to cancellation
  → Pause for 1-3 months (free)
  → Downgrade to lower tier
  → Switch to annual at discount
  → Free bonus month to give it another try

Step 3: If they still cancel
  → Confirm cancellation with grace
  → Send exit survey
  → Add to win-back sequence (30, 60, 90 days)
```

## Community Engagement

### Daily Community Management (15-30 min)

1. Welcome new members personally (or with a system)
2. Respond to questions and discussions
3. Highlight great posts or wins
4. Seed a discussion or question
5. Connect members who could help each other

### Engagement Drivers

| Driver | Example | Frequency |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| Welcome ritual | New member introduction thread | Per new member |
| Wins thread | "Share your wins this week" | Weekly |
| Hot seat / coaching | "Post your question for tomorrow's call" | Weekly |
| Challenge | "7-day [skill] challenge" | Monthly |
| Member spotlight | Feature a member's story/results | Bi-weekly |
| Resource share | Curated tools, articles, templates | Weekly |
| AMA (Ask Me Anything) | Live Q&A on specific topic | Monthly |
| Accountability | Check-in threads, partner matching | Ongoing |

## Membership Metrics

### Key Performance Indicators

| Metric | Formula | Healthy Benchmark |
|--------|---------|-------------------|
| Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) | Members × Average monthly price | Growing month over month |
| Churn Rate | Cancellations / Total members (monthly) | <5% monthly, <30% annual |
| Lifetime Value (LTV) | Average revenue per member over their lifespan | 8-15 months of dues |
| Member Acquisition Cost (MAC) | Marketing spend / New members | < 3 months of dues |
| Net Revenue Retention | Revenue from existing members / Previous period revenue | >100% (expansion > churn) |
| Engagement Rate | Active members / Total members (monthly) | >60% |
| NPS Score | Promoters - Detractors | >50 |

### The Golden Ratio
**LTV:MAC should be 3:1 or better.** If you spend $100 to acquire a member and they pay $50/month staying an average of 8 months, LTV = $400, ratio = 4:1.

## Launch Strategy for Memberships

### Founding Member Launch
1. **Announce** the membership concept to your existing audience
2. **Offer** founding member pricing (locked in forever, 30-50% discount)
3. **Limit** founding member spots (creates urgency)
4. **Launch** with a charter group and build alongside them
5. **Iterate** based on founding member feedback before public launch

### Ongoing Member Acquisition
- Lead magnet → Email sequence → Membership offer
- Webinar → Membership as the offer
- Free challenge → Membership as the next step
- Course buyers → Membership as continuity
- Podcast/content → Membership CTA
